Importing data

This dataset lists every shooting incident that happened in NYC from 2006 to 2021. Specifically, it contains information on the shooting date/time, location, and the demographic information on both perpetrators and victims.

Lets import the data first:

nypd <- read.csv("https://data.cityofnewyork.us/api/views/833y-fsy8/rows.csv?accessType=DOWNLOAD")

Data wrangling

There are 19 columns, of which Ill only need the occurrence date, boroughs, shooting outcome and the demographics of the perpetrator and victim for my analysis.

nypd <- nypd %>%

Analysis

As we can see from the graphs above, the overall trend of shooting incidents in NYC is slowly decreasing until 2018 where it took a sharp turn, increased dramatically and peaked in 2020. Across the five boroughs, Brooklyn has remained number one in terms of shooting incidents, followed by Bronx, Queens, Manhattan, and Staten Island. However, in terms of murder rate, Staten Island surpassed all the other boroughs as can be seen in the table below.

murder_rate = table(nypd$BORO, nypd$STATISTICAL_MURDER_FLAG)

Model

Ill use a logistic regression model to predict the outcome of a shooting incident based on the demographic information of the perpetrator and the victim. As shown in the output below, PERP_SEXU, PERP_AGE_GROUP25-44, PERP_AGE_GROUP45-64, PERP_AGE_GROUPUNKNOWN, VIC_AGE_GROUP18-24, VIC_AGE_GROUP25-44, VIC_AGE_GROUP45-64 and VIC_AGE_GROUP65+ are statistically significant predictors of whether a shooting crime will result in murder.

mod <- glm(STATISTICAL_MURDER_FLAG ~ PERP_RACE + PERP_SEX + PERP_AGE_GROUP + VIC_RACE + VIC_SEX + VIC_AGE_GROUP, family="binomial", data=nypd)

Identify bias

One potential source of bias in this analysis is the lack of information on population and geography of the boroughs. Brooklyn may seem to be the most dangerous neighborhood in NYC judging from the total number of shooting incidents, but we really dont know for sure until we factor in the population and land area of these boroughs. We can also question the reporting mechanism of these crimes. Black males are traditionally a very vulnerable group in the criminal justice system. There are a lot of prominent evidences as to how black males have been more targeted and mistreated in the legislative and law enforcement process. We should bear these bias in mind when analyzing and interpreting the results of how the data truly reflect the shooting crime landscape of NYC.
